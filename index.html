Hi, I am Aranyak Acharyya, a Postdoctoral fellow at Mathematical Institute for Data Science, Johns Hopkins University.

I am working with Dr. Carey E. Priebe. In 2024, I completed my PhD from the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics at Johns Hopkins University.

My research is methodological, it primarily involves proposing algorithms and proving theorems

meant to solve problems motivated from real life. My research centers around network science, artificial intelligence and high dimensional statistics. I am mostly interested in proposing methods that can capture the unknown

low dimensional structure underlying high dimensional datasets, so that reliable inference can be drawn

based on the estimated low dimensional structure.

While I am not particularly choosy about the area of application of my methods, my general interest

lies with the fields of neuroscience and social studies. I am actively looking for

Statistical analysis of generative models

Given any particular input, generative models can return a random output. In essence, generative models

are random maps from an input space (set of images, texts) to an output space (set of texts, responses).

Examples of generative models include ChatGPT and text-to-image diffusion models. Our goal in this project

is to statistically analyze generative models by studying the properties of their vector representations.

So far, we have established sufficient conditions for consistently estimating the population-level

vector representations of generative models from their sample responses. We have also established

that under certain conditions, decision-making tasks can be reliably carried out with the

sample responses from the generative models. Our further goal is to study the large sample probability distribution

of the vector representations of the generative models.

Hi, I am Aranyak Acharyya, a Postdoctoral fellow at Mathematical Institute for Data Science, Johns Hopkins University.

I am working with Dr. Carey E. Priebe. In 2024, I completed my PhD from the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics

at Johns Hopkins University.

My research is methodological, it primarily involves proposing algorithms and proving theorems

meant to solve problems motivated from real life. My research centers around network science, artificial intelligence and high

dimensional statistics. I am mostly interested in proposing methods that can capture the unknown

low dimensional structure underlying high dimensional datasets, so that reliable inference can be drawn

based on the estimated low dimensional structure.

While I am not prticularly choosy about the area of application of my methods, my general interest

lies with the fields of neuroscience and social studies. I am actively looking for

academic positions (preferably within USA) with employment periods starting from Fall 2025.
